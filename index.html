  • Range-Bound Prices:

Most of the data points stay trapped in a specific corridor between 400k and 600k, showing that the market as a whole was surprisingly stagnant over these 25 years.

Instead of a smooth trajectory, the cities exhibit a "ping-pong" motion, where prices bounce up and down independently of one another.

  • Localized Volatility: 

This suggests that home prices in this dataset were driven by short-term, city-specific factors rather than one big, synchronized economic cycle
